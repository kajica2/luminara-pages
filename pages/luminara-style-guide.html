01

The Letterform as Landscape

Luminara is an ultra-heavy, capitals-only display typeface rooted in the 1972 Novum Gebrauchsgraphik tradition. Every glyph is reduced to its most primitive geometric shape — square, circle, triangle, stadium — and then hollowed out to become a container.


The black mass defines the letter. The internal graphic defines its soul. Where traditional type relies on white space to create legibility, Luminara relies on vivid pattern, illustration, and color locked inside razor-sharp silhouettes.

04

Interior Motifs

The graphic content inside each glyph follows one of four sanctioned motifs. Assign motifs per word or per row — never mix motifs within a single letterform.

Motif A

Concentric Rainbow

Arcs of alternating palette colors that follow the inner curve of the letter. The arcs radiate outward from the glyph's center of gravity.

Motif B

Scale / Wave Pattern

Overlapping semi-circles in the Seigaiha tradition, rendered in vibrant pop colors. Rows alternate direction for optical rhythm.

Motif D

Celestial

Flat five-pointed stars and stylized bubble clouds with thick black outlines, floating on a solid-color field from the palette.

Motif C

Character Illustration

A "rubber-hose" or 70s cartoon character — elephant, devil, candle, or creature — peeking out from the bottom of the glyph. Thick black outlines, flat palette fills.

05

The Container Rule

Luminara glyphs function as clipping masks. The outer silhouette is razor-sharp — no serifs, no spurs, no terminals. Internal counter-spaces are maximized to provide the largest possible window for illustration.

06

Grid & Layout

Arrangement is as important as the letters themselves. Luminara is designed to tile into dense, poster-like compositions.

↕

Leading

Extremely tight. The top of Row 2 should nearly touch the bottom of Row 1, creating a dense vertical rhythm.

↔

Kerning

Uniform and wide. Each letter breathes as an independent object — equal spacing between all glyph pairs.

▦

Alignment

Justified or centered grid. Letters must align vertically across rows to produce a "wallpaper" tiling effect.

07

Do & Don't

✓ Do

  • Use only uppercase — Luminara has no lowercase
  • Fill glyphs with sanctioned motifs (A–D)
  • Maintain uniform, wide kerning
  • Use the five Pop palette colors inside glyphs
  • Keep outer silhouettes razor-sharp
  • Maximize counter-spaces for illustration area
  • Tile letters into dense grid compositions

✕ Don't

  • Add serifs, spurs, or decorative terminals
  • Use pastels or muted tones inside glyphs
  • Mix multiple motifs within a single letter
  • Modulate stroke width on circular forms
  • Apply drop shadows or 3D effects to silhouettes
  • Use loose leading — keep rows nearly touching
  • Set Luminara as body text — display only

08

Technical Specification

Format OTF-SVG OpenType-SVG Color Font (primary). Bakes textures and motifs directly into glyph data.
Fallback OTF Standard solid-black version for use with manual clipping masks in design software.
Case Support Capitals only. All characters are uppercase "Titling" height. No lowercase, no small caps.
Weight Black Single weight — Ultra-Heavy / Black. Stroke occupies 80–90% of bounding box.
Stroke Width No modulation. Uniform geometric strokes throughout.
Masking Behavior Glyphs function as clipping masks. External edges are crisp vector paths suitable for SVG <clipPath> or CSS clip-path.
Recommended Use Display / Headline / Poster. Minimum size 48pt. Not suitable for body text.
